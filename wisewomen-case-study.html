01 — The Problem Space

Where productivity systems break for women.

Planners optimise for task completion. They assume a flat, steady person with one role and constant energy. That person does not exist.

01
Energy is treated as constant
A to-do list assumes the same capacity every day. Energy shifts across the cycle, and the plan never accounts for it.
02
One flat list, many roles
Work, home, care, self. Tasks from every role pile into a single list with no sense of context or boundary.
03
Setup is the real cost
Most tools demand structure before they give value. The cognitive load of managing the planner becomes its own task.

05 — AI Architecture at PM Depth

Two systems, chosen for two jobs.

Text and voice do not share a pipeline. Each input type routes to the system that fits it. This is the most important technical decision in the product.

Voice to Task — LLM path
1Web Speech API captures spoken input in the browser.
2Transcript is sent to an edge function.
3Gemini parses intent and splits multiple tasks from one utterance.
4Returns a structured task array.
5Tasks render in the UI for review.
Energy Recommendations — LLM path
1Cycle phase and context are gathered.
2Sent to Gemini for interpretation.
3Two outputs: an energy read and a guidance message.
4Displayed as a phase-aware prompt, never a command.

07 — Design Process

Five days, four moves.

01
Problem Validation
Built for personal use first. Secondary research came second, to test the hypothesis rather than invent it.
02
Define Direction
Set the planning chain and the assist-not-automate principle before any screen existed.
03
Build MVP
Vibe-coded on Lovable. Rules-based scheduling over full LLM to keep the MVP stable in a five-day window.
04
Iterate & Refine
Tightened the AI routing, separated voice and text systems, and fixed tone on energy guidance.
08 — Reflections & Future Scope

What held, what fought back.

What Worked

+

The hybrid AI strategy kept the MVP stable and cheap to run.

+

A rule engine for text capture removed latency and token cost entirely.

+

The deterministic scheduler made the system predictable, so users trusted it.

+

Building for personal use first kept the scope honest and the hypothesis testable.

What Was Hard

–

Splitting voice and text into two systems took several iterations to get right.

–

Cycle-phase energy guidance needed careful tone, not just accuracy.

–

The five-day scope forced cutting richer pattern recognition.

–

Validating a personal hypothesis with limited secondary research.

–

Keeping the LLM assistive and never autonomous, by design, at every step.

Current state

Live MVP with real users. Vibe-coded on Lovable with Google OAuth and Supabase. Functional end to end: capture, categorisation, energy guidance, and scheduling.

Future scope

Native deployment, advanced pattern recognition from longer activity history, and richer cycle-signal integration to sharpen the energy read.

It does not ask her to plan harder. It plans with her.
